The Land and Housing Survey in a Global Sample of Cities

The Land and Housing Survey in a Global Sample of Cities is part of a broader research initiative—titled Monitoring Global Urban Expansion — undertaken by the NYU Stern Urbanization Project in partnership with the United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat) and the Lincoln Institute of Land Policy. The main objective of this initiative is to add a spatial dimension to monitoring global urbanization, and to support evidence-based planning and policy making in cities and metropolitan areas the world over.

The Land and Housing Survey in a Global Sample of Cities includes: 1) Survey of the Regulatory Regime Governing Land and Housing, which captures land ownership patterns, land-use planning, and development of new subdivisions in expansion areas of the city and 2) The Affordability Survey, which compiles costs of different types of land, houses, and apartments available for sale or rent in the city.

The global sample of cities includes 200 cities of different size from all world regions, 5% of the 4,000+ cities and metropolitan areas that had 100,000 people or more in 2010. The results of the study will be presented at Habitat III, the United Nations Conference on Housing and Sustainable Urban Development, now scheduled to take place in Quito, Ecuador in October 2016.
